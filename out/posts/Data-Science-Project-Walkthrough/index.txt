<p>This code calculates the residuals (differences between actual and predicted values) of a linear regression model and visualizes them as a histogram with 25 bins.</p>
<p>We visualize the residuals to check the assumptions of linear regression, specifically:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Normality</strong>: A histogram of residuals can help verify if the errors are normally distributed, which is an assumption of linear regression.</li>
<li><strong>Constant variance</strong>: A uniform spread of residuals in the histogram indicates constant variance, another assumption of linear regression.</li>
<li><strong>No patterns</strong>: A random, scattered pattern in the histogram suggests that the model is capturing the underlying relationships in the data.</li>
</ol>
<p>By examining the residual histogram, we can identify potential issues with the model, such as non-normality, heteroscedasticity, or non-linearity.</p>
