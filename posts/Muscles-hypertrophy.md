# How a muscle grow

## muscle trauma and satellite cells 
When a muscle goes under exercise the muscle fibers gets a trauma (muscle injury or damage) this damage activates satellite cells which start fusing together and to the muscles fibers often leading to hypertrophy.
satellite cells have only one nucleus it can replicate by dividing as its multiply some of the cells remain as organelles on the muscle fibers and fuse to it to form new protein stand and/or repair damage increasing the size and number of the muscle cells myofibrils, after that some satellite cells donor there nuclei to the muscle fiber leading to increase number of nuclei's in the muscle fiber meaning synthesize more proteins.
	
> [!NOTE] Interesting Note
> 
> Slow-twitch muscle fibers have more satellite cells than Fast-twitch muscle fibers in the same muscle
## Growth factors
its hormones or hormone-like
### 1- Hepatocyte growth factor (HGF):
major regulator of satellite cell activity, its the active factor in damaged muscle it is the only factor that activates quiescent satellite cells in skeletal muscle.
HGF is located in the extracellular matrix (ECM), its distribution varies in the ECM depends on the muscle fiber type
// getting older make HGF binding to the satellite cells less because of nitration by peroxynitrite

HGF distribution in the ECM of type II (fast-twitch) muscles is higher than type I (slow-twitch) muscles, that is a reason why slow-twitch muscles dont grow big even that it have more satellite cells than fast-twitch muscles

when someone stop workout for a period of time his muscles will get smaller and HGF receptors will be broken (nitration) this will inhibit satellite cell activation, but when turning back to exercises (reloading) your body will counteract nitration enabling HGF to bind with satellite cells, that is a reason why turning back to workout muscles get back to its size rapidly, but the mechanisms that counteract nitration weaken while getting older in age
### 2- Fibroblast growth factor(FGF):
important factor in muscle repair after the exercise, its role may be during muscle regeneration forming new blood capillaries.
### 3- insulin-like growth factor-I and –II (IGFs):
it plays a major role organizing the amount of muscle mass growth, promoting muscle cell repair also promoting changes in the DNA for protein synthesis.
Insulin also enhance protein synthesis and facilitate the entry of glucose into cells stimulating muscle growth.
glucose used for intramuscular energy needs also its helpful for the satellite cells enabling their cell growth activities.
### 4- Growth hormone(GH):
resistance exercises stimulate the release of GH released levels depend on exercise intensity, GH is used to release the stored energy in fat cells for the muscle growth process, also GH stimulate the uptake and incorporation of amino acids into protein in skeletal muscles.
### 5- testosterone:
it stimulate GH responses, also it increase the number of neurotransmitters in the fiber site leading to gain strength also help to activate tissue growth 

> [!warning]+ important Note
> Muscle growth occurs only when the rate of muscle protein synthesis is greater than the rate of muscle protein breakdown.
