# what is it and how to achieve it

Basically, a self-sustainable aquarium ecosystem means that the fish tank must be able to run itself. It shouldn't need any intervention from you like filters, pumps, or any type of fish feeding. So, you don't have to feed anything in the tank, and you don't even need an automatic feeder.

how a sustainable aquarium works?

## 1- PLANTS
	This is the most important part of the system; basically because **plants are the water filter**. They absorb the
	waste produced by the fish, keeping the water clean.
	You need _a lot_ of plants, specifically fast-growing ones, to handle the filtration job.
	
## 2- LIGHTING
	the plants need a light source to grow, there must be light 5-8 hours a day to mimic sun light
	the light must not be strong or weak it must be in between, weak light can cause some plants to die or grow very slow
	while strong light can damage some and cause algae to bloom, to mimic the sun light perfectly get a full Spectrum
	Light
	
## 3- SOIL
	for the plants that need soil because there is some plants can live without soil sense we need a lot of plants we cant
	just relay on plants that dont need soil, also it lowers the PH level to about 6.5 which is very good for most plants
	and fishes.
	you can use garden dirt instead but its harder.
	
## 4- SNAILS AND SHRIMPS
	snails favorite food is algae they eat it 24/7 cleaning the glass removing algae it must be in the tank in the first
	stages before the algae eat the tank, also its waste is a good fertilizer for soil.
	the shrimps is the real goat, because of its benefits in the ecosystem it suppresses algae blooms specially
	filamentous and enhances water quality through organic matter processing and nutrient recycling
	
## 5- ZOOPLANKTON
	this is the fish food, its eggs will come with the plants so no need to buy it, but we can make there food at home, to
	make it take any fruit peel or lettuce put it in water and put it in a place have sun but not burning sun wait until
	the water becomes slightly white these are small creatures called Infusoria its the food for zooplankton.
	also adding a Catappa leaf like Indian almond leaf it have a lot of benefits beside providing food for zooplankton it
	provide food for snails and shrimps specially at the first (biofilm), it makes PH level better, and it provide
	something called tannins it kills the harmful bacteria.
	
## 6- FISHES
	Now after everything is set, we can add some fishes.
	In a small tank, it's hard to have Apex Predators because the tank will not provide enough food for them or there 
	bioload will be too high.
	Adding fishes like **Guppy** or **Endlers** is very good. Even though they breed very fast, their numbers will not
	exceed the limits because there will not be enough food for all of them. The survival rate of newborn ones will be low
	(parents will eat them), keeping the population stable.


this is a simple ecosystem need a very low human intervention like adding water because the water will evaporate.
